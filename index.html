// The Mechanism

HOW WE FIND BUYERS BEFORE THEY RAISE THEIR HAND

Most data is context. We measure deviation. There's a massive difference between what someone looks at and when their looking habits suddenly change.

01

Build the Baseline

For every profile in our 280M+ identity graph, we calculate a long-term average engagement score for each topic. This is their "normal." Everyone has one.

02

Monitor the 7-Day Window

Every week, we re-process 250 billion+ URLs to measure whether each person's recent activity is deviating upward from their baseline — not just whether they're active.

03

Flag the Deviation

When a person's 7-day score spikes significantly above their personal baseline, they're flagged as entering an active buying cycle — with a 0–100 intent strength score assigned.

04

Deliver the Lead

You get a list of real people — name, email, phone, address, demographics — whose behavior changed this week. Segmented by vertical, geography, and intent score. Ready to contact.

// Straight Answers

THE QUESTIONS
YOU'RE THINKING.

How is this different from just buying a list of people who visited competitor websites? +
Competitor visit data tells you what someone looked at once. We tell you when someone's behavior significantly deviated from their personal baseline over a 7-day window. The first is context. The second is intent. Visiting a roofing site doesn't make you a buyer. Tripling your roofing research activity this week does. That's the Colin signal.
How fresh is the data? +
The behavioral intent feed is reprocessed from 250 billion+ URLs every single week. Email verification runs at 10 million records per day. Phone numbers are skip-traced against 10–20 offline datasets and rebuilt every few weeks. You're not buying data from six months ago. You're buying behavior that changed this week.
Is this compliant? TCPA? CAN-SPAM? +
The data is compliantly sourced. DNC flags are built directly into every record so you're never calling someone who's opted out of outbound calls. Your responsibility as the end-user is to honor those flags, include proper opt-outs in email, and follow standard TCPA / CAN-SPAM practice — the same rules you already follow. We give you the tools. Compliance is a two-way street.
What does a list actually look like? What fields do I get? +
Every record includes: first and last name, personal email (ESP-validated), mobile phone (skip-traced, DNC-flagged), physical address, age range, income range, net worth estimate, homeowner status, and intent score. B2B lists additionally include company name, business email, job title, LinkedIn URL, and company revenue. Everything your sales team needs to have a real conversation — not just a name and a prayer.
We already use Apollo and ZoomInfo. Why would we need this? +
Apollo and ZoomInfo tell you who exists. We tell you who's buying — right now. They're static databases. We're a live behavioral signal. Ask yourself: are those tools showing you when someone's behavior actually changed, or just that the person exists in a category? If your list is based on job title + company size, you're calling everyone. We help you call the right ones at the right moment.
What's the minimum commitment? +
We'll discuss volume, cadence, and structure on our intro call. There's no one-size-fits-all here — a roofing company running a 3-person sales team needs something different than an insurance carrier running 50 agents. Book 15 minutes and we'll map out what actually makes sense for your business before you commit to anything.
